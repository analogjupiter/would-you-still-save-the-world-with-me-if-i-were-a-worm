Game Guide to “Would you still save the world with me if I were a worm?”

Would you still save the world with me if I were a worm? is a puzzle game created for the 4MB Jam 2025.

WARNING: MAJOR SPOILER AHEAD

Basic gameplay loop

The game is controlled by mouse.

The field of play is a rectangular grid of 21×11 tiles.

The main protagonist of the game is “your partner” represented by a worm. Left-clicking on a position within the field of play, places a marker that your partner will walk towards to — as far as possible. There is no path finding, the character will always walk in a straigt line until it hits an obstacle.

One has to walk to the exit, picking up collectibles, avoiding obstacles and enemies; potentially teleporting one’s way through wormholes.

Entities

The main objective is to reach the exit wormhole of each puzzle; displayed as a cyclone with a chequered flag. In later levels that contain collectibles — in the form of apples — the exit wormhole is only usable after all collectibles have been acquired. Such an locked exit is signified by a lock instead of a chequered flag.

The collectibles are either red or green apples, depending on the “region” of the level.

Rocks and (regular) holes block the path and should be avoided. While rocks act like a wall, holes trap the player and cause the level to be reset — with all character placed in their initial positions.

Similar to the exit wormhole, there are also regular wormholes that act as a teleportation mechanism. There is always two of them that are linked to each other. Stepping onto one, teleports the player to the other. One does not have to walk off and back onto a them to travel through it; instead wormholes can be retriggered by placing a marker at the current location.

Wormholes also serve as a safe place. Due to the bend in gravitational forces occuring on them, no enemies can attack a player standing on either end of a wormhole tunnel.

Turtles serve as the main enemies during the puzzles. They appear in later levels and have to be avoided as they will try to eat the player. Getting eaten by a turtles has equivalent effects to falling into a hole; the player is recovered to their start position and the whole level is reset.

Turtles move around the field and are big enough to step over holes. Rocks, however, block their way.

Avoiding turtles

Turtles are particularly annoying because they cannot be defeated.

Luckily, they have severe weak spots. First of all, turtles one move when the player moves. They can walk up to one field per field that the player goes.

Furthermore, they can only ever walk into four directions: left, right, up and down. This is unlike the player — who can also perform diagonal movements. Combining these facts leads to a tactic where cells that are only diagonal neighbors to turtles can not be occupied by a turtle after the upcoming move.
